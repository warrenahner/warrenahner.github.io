**App:** OpenTranscript
**Developer:** Warren Ahner (individual developer)
**Effective date:** June 4, 2026
**Last updated:** June 4, 2026

## Summary

OpenTranscript is a free app built for the community. It does **not** collect,
track, sell, or share any personal information. There are no analytics, no
advertising, no third-party trackers, and no user accounts. Everything the app
does happens **on your device**.

If you read nothing else, read this: **I do not collect any data about you.**

## Who I am

OpenTranscript is developed and published by Warren Ahner as an individual,
independent developer. I am the sole party responsible for the app. I am not a
company, and I do not operate any servers that receive your data.

## Information I collect

**None.**

The app does not collect, store on any server, or transmit:

- Personal information (name, email address, phone number, contacts).
- Account or login information — there are no accounts.
- Location data.
- Advertising identifiers (IDFA) or any device identifiers used for tracking.
- Usage analytics, crash analytics, or telemetry.
- Cookies or similar tracking technologies.

I have not integrated any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs
into the app.

## How the app works (on-device processing)

OpenTranscript performs its work locally on your device. To provide its core
features, the app may request your permission to access the **microphone** so it
can record and transcribe audio.

- Audio you record and the transcripts the app produces are processed **on your
  device**.
- This content is **not** uploaded to me or to any third party, and it never
  leaves your device through the app.
- Any recordings, transcripts, or related files are stored locally on your
  device and remain under your control.

Because this processing happens on-device, I never see, receive, or have access
to your recordings or transcripts.

## How your data is stored and deleted

Any content the app creates is stored locally on your device. You can delete this
content at any time from within the app, and you can remove all app data by
deleting the app from your device. Standard iOS backups (such as iCloud Backup or
encrypted device backups) are controlled by your own device and iCloud settings,
not by me, and are subject to Apple's privacy practices.

## Permissions the app may request

The app may ask for the following iOS permission, which you can grant or deny at
any time in **Settings → Privacy & Security**:

- **Microphone** — used solely to record and transcribe audio on your device.

The app only requests permissions needed for features you choose to use, and it
functions without sending any data off the device.

## Third-party services

OpenTranscript does not use third-party advertising networks, analytics
providers, or data brokers. The app is distributed through Apple's App Store;
Apple may collect certain information as part of operating the App Store (for
example, download and purchase records) under
[Apple's Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/). I do not receive
any personally identifying information from these processes.

## Children's privacy

OpenTranscript does not collect any personal information from anyone, including
children. The app does not knowingly collect data from children under 13 (or the
equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction) because it does not collect personal
data from any user.

## Your rights

Because I do not collect or hold any of your personal data, there is nothing for
me to access, correct, export, or delete on your behalf. You remain in full
control of all content on your own device. Depending on where you live, you may
have rights under laws such as the GDPR or CCPA; those rights are satisfied here
by the fact that no personal data is collected or processed by me.

## Changes to this policy

If I make changes to this Privacy Policy, I will update the "Last updated" date
above and post the revised policy at this same URL. Continued use of the app
after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
